+++ ESP8266 ESP-12E NodeMCU pins

Label   Pin:GPIO
D0      16          Tested with button to turn LED on or off.
D1      05
D2      04
D3      00
D4(TX)  02          Built in, on board LED
3V      3v output
G       Ground
D5      14
D6      12          Out to a resister, to an LED, to ground.
D7(RX)  13          Infrared receive: input (Didn't work on D8 which is TX)
D8(TX)  15
RX      03
TX      01
G       Ground      Infrared receive: ground
3V      3v output   Infrared receive: power

GPIO (GP: General Purpose, IO: Input/Output)

Tested: button output using D0-D02

+++ To develop and test

+ Start the webserver which is used for the NodeMCU to make an HTTP request.
$ node webserver

+ Start the Arduino IDE.
+ Open program: HttpGetSync.ino.
+ Enter the WiFI password. Note, after updates, before uploading the repository, remove the password.

+ Plug the NodeMCU into the computer's USB device. For my USB device, I click the USB On button.
+ Open the serial monitor from the IDE: Tools/Serial Monitor.

+ Watch the monitor. Example:
+ Connecting to the WiFi network: BATCAVE
